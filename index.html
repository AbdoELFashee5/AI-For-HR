Introduction to AI in HR — The Unfair Advantage
Why AI is the most important skill shift in HR since HRIS systems. First live win in 15 minutes.
90 min
Foundation
›
The Pain
HR professionals spend 57–73% of their time on administrative tasks — writing the same emails, formatting the same documents, answering the same policy questions — instead of strategic people work. This is the problem AI solves immediately.
The Cure
In the first 15 minutes, every participant takes a messy, informal job requirement note from their manager and produces a fully polished, bias-reduced job description using one prompt. They see the result before we explain the theory.

What we cover

  • The HR-specific AI opportunity: what tasks eat the most hours and which ones AI eliminates first
  • Live demonstration: rough manager note → polished JD in 90 seconds (the hook)
  • What is an LLM, explained in plain HR terms — it is a pattern-completing engine, not a search engine
  • The 3 tools we will use throughout: ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini — when to use each
  • Safety and confidentiality: what to never put into a free AI tool, and how Teams/Enterprise plans solve that
  • The 6-Part Prompt Framework introduced (Task, Context, Specifications, Persona, Examples, Notes) — the core skill we will use in every single lesson

First live prompt (done together in the room)

Prompt Template — Job Description Generator
You are a senior talent acquisition specialist with 15 years of experience writing inclusive, compelling job descriptions. Task: Turn the rough notes below into a polished job description for our careers page. Context: We are GlobalHR Solutions, a 500-person professional services firm. We are hiring for [role]. The hiring manager sent these notes: [paste raw notes here]. Specifications: Write in a professional but human tone. Include: role summary (3 sentences), key responsibilities (6 bullet points), required qualifications, preferred qualifications, and a 2-sentence company culture statement. Remove any language that could signal gender or age bias. Keep total length under 400 words. Notes: Do not invent responsibilities not mentioned in the notes. If anything is unclear, flag it at the end.

Recruitment Part 1 — Sourcing, Screening & Candidate Communication
From 200 CVs to a ranked shortlist in minutes. Mass communication done in seconds.
90 min
Recruitment
›
The Pain
A typical HR specialist reads 150–300 CVs per role opening. At 3 minutes per CV that is 7.5–15 hours of screen time per hire — for a single role. Then they write individual outreach and rejection emails one by one.
The Cure
Build a scoring rubric with AI in 2 minutes. Paste each CV text and get a structured score and recommendation in 15 seconds. Write all 5 outreach email variants in one prompt. The entire intake process for a role drops from 2 days to 3 hours.

What we cover

  • Building a role-specific scoring rubric: give AI the JD and ask it to define weighted criteria (experience, skills, culture signals)
  • The CV screening method: copy-paste CV text → AI outputs a score, a 3-line summary, and a hire/hold/reject recommendation
  • Batch screening workflow: how to process 20 CVs in a single structured session without losing context
  • Writing the full outreach communication suite: interview invite, scheduling confirmation, hold email, rejection, and counter-offer response — all five in one 90-second prompt
  • The refinement cycle applied to screening: ask AI to criticize its own shortlist and check for overlooked candidates

Core prompt templates

Prompt 1 — Build a Scoring Rubric
You are a senior talent acquisition specialist. Based on the job description below, create a scoring rubric I can use to evaluate CVs consistently. Job description: [paste JD] Output: A table with 6 criteria, a weight (%) for each totaling 100%, what a strong / average / weak candidate looks like for each criterion, and red-flag signals to watch for. Keep it practical enough to use in 2 minutes per CV.
Prompt 2 — Screen a Single CV
Using the rubric below, evaluate this candidate's CV. Rubric: [paste rubric] CV: [paste CV text] Output: Score out of 100, a 3-line summary of their fit, their strongest point, their biggest gap, and a clear recommendation: Advance / Hold / Reject.

Recruitment Part 2 — Chatting with Documents & Policy Knowledge Base
Upload your HR policy manual. Ask it questions in plain language. Get answers in 5 seconds instead of 30 minutes.
60 min
Recruitment
›
The Pain
Every HR professional spends 20–40 minutes per week hunting through policy documents, labor law PDFs, and employee handbooks to answer questions they've answered a hundred times before. The document exists — finding the right paragraph does not.
The Cure
Upload the policy document, employee handbook, or labor law PDF directly into the chat. Then ask questions in plain language — "Is a 3-month probation standard for this role?" or "What is our policy on remote work for employees who have been here less than 6 months?" — and get the exact paragraph reference and a plain-language summary in seconds.

What we cover

  • How to upload PDF/Word documents and query them conversationally — the "document chat" technique
  • Building a personal HR knowledge assistant: upload all relevant policy docs to a ChatGPT Project or Claude Project and give it persistent context
  • The interrogation pattern applied to policy: ask AI what it needs to give you the most accurate policy answer before you ask the actual question
  • Chatbot-style knowledge base demo: how HR can create a simple "ask me anything about our policies" assistant using a custom GPT loaded with company documents
  • What to do when documents conflict or are outdated: AI flags contradictions and requests clarification

Core prompt template

Prompt — Document Policy Assistant Setup
You are an HR policy specialist for GlobalHR Solutions. I have uploaded our employee handbook, HR policies manual, and applicable labor law summary. Your job is to answer HR questions accurately, cite the exact policy section that applies, and flag any contradictions or gaps you notice. Before answering any policy question, always: (1) state which document your answer comes from, (2) give a plain-language summary, and (3) flag if the situation might require legal counsel. When you don't know, say so clearly rather than guessing.

Performance Management — AI-Assisted Reviews & Bias Reduction
Draft a full performance review in 3 minutes. Detect bias patterns across 50 reviews in seconds.
90 min
Performance
›
The Pain
Performance review seasons consume 2–4 hours per manager per employee — and the results are often vague, inconsistent, and inadvertently biased. HR collects 200 reviews that use different language, different standards, and tell them nothing useful about organizational patterns.
The Cure
Managers give AI 5 bullet points of raw observations. AI produces a structured, specific, behavior-based review that eliminates gendered language, rates-the-behavior-not-the-person, and meets company policy standards. HR then pastes all 200 reviews into a single session and gets a cross-departmental pattern analysis in minutes.

What we cover

  • The performance review generator: raw manager notes → structured, bias-checked, behavior-based review with specific examples and development recommendations
  • The bias-detection prompt: paste any review and ask AI to flag vague language, personality-based rather than behavior-based criticism, and gendered or age-related language patterns
  • Cross-department pattern analysis: paste multiple reviews and ask AI to identify which teams are receiving inflated ratings, which managers are under-documenting, and what themes appear among low performers
  • Calibration preparation: use AI to build a calibration meeting agenda based on submitted ratings — who needs discussion, where are the outliers
  • The 7-phase workflow applied: input the review data → organize by department → analyze patterns → confirm with manager input → communicate findings to leadership

Core prompt templates

Prompt 1 — Performance Review Generator
You are a senior HR business partner specializing in performance management and constructive feedback. Task: Write a complete, professional performance review for the employee below based on the manager's raw observations. Context: Employee: [name], [role], [department]. Review period: [dates]. Manager observations: [paste bullet points]. Key objectives for the period: [list OKRs or goals]. Rating scale used: [1–5 or Exceeds/Meets/Below]. Specifications: Write in third person. Base every statement on observable behavior, not personality. Include: overall performance summary (2 sentences), key achievements (3–4 specific examples), areas for development (2 specific and actionable), and a recommended rating with justification. Flag any of the manager's observations that are too vague to include. Run a final bias check and note anything you removed or reworded.
Prompt 2 — Bias Detection Scan
Review the following performance review text for bias, vagueness, and policy non-compliance. Check for: (1) gendered language or stereotyping, (2) age-related references, (3) personality-based rather than behavior-based statements, (4) vague qualifiers without examples ("sometimes," "generally"), (5) any language that could expose the company to discrimination risk. For each issue found: quote the exact phrase, explain why it is problematic, and suggest a replacement. End with an overall compliance rating: Green / Amber / Red.

Predictive Analytics — Flight Risk, Retention & Sentiment
Stop losing good people to surprises. Use data you already have to identify who is at risk before they hand in their notice.
90 min
Performance
›
The Pain
The average cost of replacing an employee is 50–200% of their annual salary. By the time HR knows someone is unhappy enough to leave, it is usually too late. The data that would have predicted it — absenteeism, performance trends, promotion wait time, survey responses — was always there.
The Cure
Use the 7-phase AI workflow on employee data: input tenure, performance trajectory, absenteeism, time since last promotion, survey sentiment, and manager relationship quality. AI identifies the pattern profile of employees who left in the last 2 years and maps current employees to that risk profile. Reactive HR becomes proactive HR.

What we cover

  • Phase 1 (Data Input): what signals to collect — tenure, absenteeism, performance trend, survey results, compensation vs. market, promotion history
  • Phase 2 (Organization): structuring the data in a format AI can analyze — building a simple anonymized profile table
  • Phase 3 (Analysis): the flight risk prompt — give AI the historical leaver profiles and ask it to identify the pattern, then apply that pattern to current employees
  • Phase 4 (Confirmation): cross-checking AI's flight risk flag list with manager input before any action is taken
  • Phase 5 (Decision): predictive scenario modeling — what happens if we promote this person? If we increase compensation? If we change their manager?
  • Sentiment analysis: paste anonymized survey responses and ask AI to surface the top 5 themes, emotional tone, and specific language clusters

Core prompt template

Prompt — Flight Risk Pattern Analysis
You are a people analytics specialist. I am going to give you two datasets. First, profiles of employees who voluntarily left GlobalHR Solutions in the last 2 years. Second, profiles of our current employees. Your task is to identify the common pattern in the leavers and then flag which current employees match that pattern most closely. Leaver profiles: [paste anonymized table — tenure, last performance rating, time since promotion, absenteeism score, last survey sentiment] Current employee profiles: [paste same format table] Output: (1) The 3–5 strongest predictors of departure you can identify from the leaver data. (2) A ranked list of current employees by risk level (High / Medium / Low) with the specific signals driving each rating. (3) For the top 5 high-risk employees, suggest one targeted retention action for each. Note: treat this as a probability model, not a certainty — flag your confidence level.

Ethical Considerations — Bias, Privacy, Compliance & Responsible Use
The lesson that makes this course safe to implement. Understanding where AI creates risk and how to manage it.
60 min
Ethics
›
The Risk
Using AI in hiring without understanding its bias profile can expose companies to discrimination liability. Using free AI tools with employee personal data can violate GDPR and local data protection law. HR professionals who use AI without this knowledge become a liability, not an asset.
The Framework
This lesson transforms HR professionals from passive AI users into responsible AI supervisors. They learn exactly where the risks live, how to use AI itself to audit for bias, which data classifications should never enter a public AI tool, and how to document AI-assisted decisions in a way that satisfies audit and compliance requirements.

What we cover

  • How AI bias originates: training data reflects historical patterns — what this means for hiring, pay equity, and promotion decisions
  • The three places bias enters HR AI use: the prompt itself, the training data of the model, and the way outputs are interpreted
  • Using AI to audit its own bias: the self-criticism technique applied to HR decisions — ask AI to argue the other side, ask it to find the flaw in its own recommendation
  • Data classification for HR: what is safe to put into public AI (anonymized structural data, policy text), what requires an Enterprise plan (employee names, performance data, salaries), what should never go in at all (medical information, immigration status)
  • GDPR and local compliance: the difference between processing, storing, and using employee data with AI — and how to maintain a defensible audit trail
  • Documenting AI-assisted decisions: how to frame a hiring decision that AI supported, in language that is defensible to a tribunal or regulator

Core prompt — bias self-audit

Prompt — AI Decision Bias Audit
I am going to share an AI-generated hiring recommendation. Your task is to act as an independent auditor and challenge it. Recommendation: [paste AI recommendation] Role being hired for: [role title] Criteria used: [paste rubric] Audit for: (1) Any criterion that could systematically disadvantage a protected group. (2) Any language in the recommendation that reflects demographic assumptions rather than job-relevant factors. (3) Any missing criteria that would have been relevant. (4) An overall bias risk rating: Low / Medium / High, with justification. Be direct and specific — this is a compliance exercise.

Interactive Workshop — Build Your Own AI-Powered HR Workflow
Each participant picks a real HR challenge from their job and runs it through the complete 7-phase AI workflow. Leave with a working deliverable.
120 min
Workshop
›
The Goal
Every participant leaves with a real, finished work product — not a hypothetical exercise. They prove to themselves that the full workflow works on their actual job, with their actual data, in one session.

Workshop structure

  • Choose your challenge (5 min): participants select one of — a hiring cycle they are currently running, an upcoming performance review season task, an onboarding gap they want to fix, or a retention problem in their team
  • Phase 1 (15 min): gather the data — upload documents, paste context, set up the 6-part prompt with custom instructions
  • Phase 2 (10 min): organize and clarify — use the Book Framework to structure the problem and build the outline
  • Phase 3 (20 min): analyze — apply the refinement cycle, generate hypotheses, ask AI to self-criticize
  • Phase 4 (10 min): confirm — cross-check with a peer in the room, use the bias audit prompt on any hiring-related output
  • Phase 5 (15 min): decide — predictive scenario modeling on the top 3 options
  • Phase 6 (15 min): communicate — produce the final deliverable: a policy draft, a hiring recommendation, a review, or a retention action plan
  • Group share (30 min): 3–4 volunteers present their workflow and output to the group. Instructor provides feedback and shows alternative approaches.

Expected deliverables from participants

Option AA complete job description + scored shortlist for a live role
Option BA 30-60-90 day plan for an incoming hire
Option CA performance review + bias audit for a real employee
Option DA flight risk analysis + retention action plan for one team
Fictional company throughout: GlobalHR Solutions — 500-person professional services firm. All scenarios, data, and personas are grounded in this one company for narrative continuity.
Tools used: ChatGPT (Teams plan), Claude, Gemini 2.0 for video/audio, Gamma.app for presentations
